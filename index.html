about me

I am currently a student in Pittsburgh, PA. My main academic areas are in Linguistics, Chinese Philosophy, and Continental Poststructural Philosophy. In Linguistics, I am currently focuses mainly on pragmatics, discourse analysis, and historical linguistics. Outside of Linguistics, which I see as a more unsullied study, my philosophical endevours can draw from a very wide range of sources and cover a very wide range of topics. Thus, my interests and knowledge in philosophy also extends to Legal Philosophy, Jewish Philosophy/Theology/Kabbalah, Schopenhauer, New Confucianism, the Kyoto School, Zen Buddhism, Radical and Postmodern Feminism, Formal Epistemology, and ancient Near Eastern Studies. I practice tea ceremony, calligraphy, and I am also a Guzhengist.
